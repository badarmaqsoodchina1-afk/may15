Short Bio

Hafiz Muhammad Sanaullah Badar is a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the School of Computer Science and Information Engineering, Henan University, China. His research interests span cybersecurity, trustworthy AI, federated learning, IoT security, and systems security for cyber-physical and distributed systems, with a focus on securing critical infrastructures and large-scale digital environments.

He has published 27 research papers, including multiple first-author contributions in leading Q1 venues such as IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, IEEE Transactions on Consumer Electronics, and IEEE Internet of Things Journal. His research spans system and software security, authentication and access control, privacy-preserving machine learning, and adversarially robust AI. He has strong expertise in designing and formally verifying security protocols, analysing vulnerabilities in distributed and resource-constrained systems, and developing resilient AI-driven security mechanisms under realistic threat models.

His current research focuses on LLM-driven malware detection, zero-day attack analysis, and AI-based vulnerability discovery, alongside federated and privacy-preserving learning for distributed environments.

Research Areas

  • Cybersecurity Protocols and Formal Security: authentication, access control, key agreement, PUF-based schemes, formal verification (ProVerif, AVISPA), and privacy-by-design architectures for smart grids and IoT.
  • Trustworthy and Robust AI: adversarial robustness (poisoning/evasion), intrusion detection, secure ML pipelines, and degradation-aware evaluation under realistic threat models.
  • Malware Analysis and Threat Intelligence: analysis of malicious behaviour, zero-day attack patterns, and AI/LLM-driven techniques for vulnerability discovery, exploit generation, and detection of advanced and previously unseen threats in software and networked systems.
  • Efficient Transformer Systems and Edge Intelligence: transformer backbones, fine-tuning, lightweight model optimisation (latency/FLOPs), deployment-aware AI for IoT and cyber-physical systems.
  • Large Language Models and Code Intelligence: representation learning for code and binaries, semantics-preserving transformations, and LLM-driven security-relevant software analysis.
  • Federated and Privacy-Preserving Learning: secure aggregation, differential privacy, homomorphic encryption, and Byzantine-robust federated learning for distributed IoT and smart grid environments.
